Sweden-based jazz and soul musician John Venkiah was born in 1986 in North London. He started to play the keyboard at 5 years of age, and moved to Sweden in 1998. Playing mostly by ear, John listened to records his mother played for him, and learnt a lot by playing what he heard.


As he grew into his teens, both singing and playing became a bigger part of his life. John began writing his own music at the age of 14, experimenting with different influences, including soul, Motown and jazz. He began studying music at high school, where he met fellow musicians and started his first bands.


John studied jazz for two years at a pre-university college where he gaining more passion, knowledge and experience within jazz. In 2012 he took his Bachelor’s degree in jazz piano at the Malmö Academy of Music. During this period, John took lessons for the great swedish pianist Jan Lundgren, who, apart from teaching him lots of fundamental things within jazz piano, helped him to move forward within jazz in many aspects.


John Venkiah is, apart from his own projects, a member of several acclaimed music groups, including Faela and Claudia Campagnol, and has shared the stage with many well known artists. He has toured, written and recorded music continuously throughout recent years, from the biggest stages in town-festivals to hotel bars and jazz clubs.


John received the Ystad Sweden Jazz Festival Next Generation Award in 2020.


Musical influences include Keith Jarrett, Brad Mehldau, Marvin Gaye, Frank Sinatra, Sam Cooke, Ella Fitzgerald, Chet Baker, Stevie Wonder, Donny Hathaway, and more.
